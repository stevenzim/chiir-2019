Experiment Instructions

Welcome to the experiment. In the experiment you will make decisions about the effectiveness of treatments for various health concerns. A total of 10 questions and decisions (plus an additional 2 practice questions with decisions) will be made in the experiment. After all decisions have been submitted we will take you to a survey to collect demographic and other information.

For some of the questions, you will be provided with a webpage mimicking results of commonly used search engines. In these results, you can click on the links (which take you to an image of the webpage). When provided a search engine results page, we ask that you imagine that you are using a search engine to find information to make your decision. Once you are ready to make a decision, you will click on a button that takes you to a decision page

For some questions, you will be provided with no search results and will be taken directly to the decision page.

You will have a chance to practice with both methods.

Before and after each medical question and decision, you will be asked a couple of questions.

When provided a search engine results page, we ask that you imagine that you are using a search engine to find an answer to this question. Once you are ready to make a decision, you will click on a button that takes you to a decision page.

For all questions and the practice questions, you will have three options available for your decisions: helpful, does not help or inconclusive.

DEFINITIONS of medical conditions and treatment are provided to you throughout the experiment. We also provide you with definitions of the 3 decision options.

helpful: The medical treatment helps if the treatment is effective and has a direct positive influence on the specified illness.

inconclusive: The effectiveness of a medical treatment is inconclusive if medical professionals are still unsure if the treatment will have a positive, negative or no influence on the specified illness.

does not help: The medical treatment does not help if the treatment is ineffective and either has no effect or has a direct negative influence on the specified illness.
